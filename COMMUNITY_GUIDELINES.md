# ByteQuant Community Guidelines

ByteQuant's community space is for reproducible, privacy-safe workflows, tool tips, method questions, and constructive feature ideas. The public source repository and GitHub Discussions provide identity, reporting, moderation, and change history without creating a separate ByteQuant account database.

## Share methods, not private data

- Never publish real personal data, customer records, passwords, API tokens, private keys, active malware, or confidential company content.
- Use synthetic examples and placeholders such as `[CUSTOMER]`, `[EMAIL]`, and `[ORDER_ID]`.
- Share only material you own or are authorized to publish.
- A ByteQuant recipe URL is public information. Exclude inputs unless they are synthetic and intended for publication.

## Be useful and respectful

- Include the goal, input shape, steps tried, expected result, and any reproducible error.
- Explain limitations and avoid claiming that a heuristic, score, or clean scan is a legal or security guarantee.
- No harassment, threats, hate speech, spam, deceptive links, or instructions intended to harm people or systems.
- Keep criticism specific to observable behavior and suggest a verifiable improvement where possible.

## Moderation and security

The on-device community composer is a limited pre-filter, not a moderation guarantee. Public posts remain subject to GitHub's terms, acceptable-use policies, reporting tools, and repository moderation. Maintainers may edit, lock, label, or remove material that violates these guidelines.

Do not publish security vulnerabilities in Discussions or Issues. Follow [SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md) for coordinated disclosure and allow reasonable time for assessment and remediation.

## Licensing contributions

Code contributions must be compatible with the repository's MIT License. Do not contribute copied proprietary code, restricted datasets, or content that cannot be used commercially. By submitting a contribution, you confirm that you have the right to provide it under the repository's terms.
